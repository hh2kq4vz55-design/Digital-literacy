Part A – Case Study (Phishing Attack)


Phishing is a cybercrime where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information like passwords or bank details. A common scenario involves a student receiving an email that appears to be from their bank or a trusted service.


1.The attacker sends a fake email with a urgent message (e.g., “Your account will be blocked”).
2.The email contains a link to a fake website that looks real.
3.The victim enters login or OTP details.
4.The attacker captures the data and misuses it for fraud.


Students and young users are often targeted because they frequently use digital platforms but may lack awareness. The consequences include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized account access
